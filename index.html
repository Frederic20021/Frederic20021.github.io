Introduction

Hello, I am an international student studying at an undergraduate course majoring in Computer Science at Shizuoka University. The faculty is located in Hamamatsu, which is a city well-known for its affluent industries.
With this post, I aim to share my experience on how I prepared and got admitted into this University. While at the same time, keeping updates on my latest experience as an international student here in Japan. I hope you will find this post useful.

Main Types of Study in Japan Plans

Through Japanese Language School

  If you want to study at a Japanese University but are not confident in your Japanese language, this method is the most suitable for you. In fact, it is the most typical way for many people planning to studying abroad in Japan. The langauge schools not only teaches you Japanese language, but they also help you prepare for various exam... preparations such as JLPT and EJU which is fairly difficult to prepare on your own. In addition to academic purposes, Language schools are also attened by those who wish to look for a job in Japan.
  However, it will typically require 2 years to complete your language studies at these language schools if you've just started learning Japanese. And if you combine an additional of 4 years (undergraduate course) of study at university, a total of 6 years would have been spent in your pursuit of undergraduate study, assuming that every step went smoothly.

Continuing your current studies in Japan

  Many Japanese Universities (undergraduate) have direct admission to the third year, allowing students to continue their studies without having to start all over again. Thus, it is the ideal way of studying abroad in Japan without...having to put in additional years of study.
  But most of the direct admission courses (undergraduate) are only offered in Japanese and the shift in language may affect your study. Therefore, it requires you to have decent level of Japanese Language to properly continue on with your studies.

Starting as a first year student

  This study plan is suitable for those expecting to graduate high school and wish to continue their study in Japan. Similar to the above plan, it is both financially and time efficient wtihout having to repeat your studies. In addition, most of the undergraduate ...schools have language programs along side with the major course studies. Thus, you would not have to worry much about the Japanese Languages.
  However, it doesn't mean that you can start with zero level of Japanese. The admission in this type of study plan usually requires a minimum of N3 Japanese language Level.
